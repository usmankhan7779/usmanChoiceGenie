Frequently Asked Questions about Choice Genie

We'll try our best to answer your queries as soon as possible.


1: What if I regularly monitor the electricity usage?

It is unlikely that by manually monitoring you would be able to get the same results as our machine learning algorithm. ChoiceGenie regularly monitors usage, constantly runs it against market’s availability rates, and makes the changes with 100% accuracy.

2: Can I go for a commercial account?

You can sign up for commercial services on our website. We will reach out to you and take the process from there.

3: Whom do I contact if there is a power outage?

If there is a power outage you should immediately call your utility – they are in charge of the delivery of electricity to your home.

4: Who will answer all the billing questions?

Your electric company is the best option for bill related queries.

5: What will happen if my retail electric provider goes out of business?

When a provider goes out of business suddenly without notice, you are automatically switched to the Provider of Last Resort (POLR).

6: In the case of nonpayment, will I be notified prior to termination?

Yes, you will receive a 10 days notice to pay your bill.

7: What is your charge rate?

Charge rate depends on your location. Enter your ZIP code and select a electricity rate for your home from the number of electricity providers.

8: How do I know who has the cheapest electricity?

Enter you ZIP code and find out who provides the cheapest electricity rates in your area.

9: How do I choose an electric company?

Choose your electric company based on: term length, plan type, price factors. You also have an option of choosing between green energy plan and add-on.

10: How do I purchase a renewable energy plan?

Enter your ZIP code and use the advanced search filter mechanism for green energy plans.

11: Do we have a contract?

No, you are not bound by a contract. You may cancel your service with us at any time.

12: Will I have a contract with the electricity provider?

We enroll you in electricity plans for as minimum a contract as 6-months. There may be factors involved making the contracts shorter or longer, but often times more than 12-months at a time.

13: How often do you switch?

We typically switch our members every 6 months. Depending on the factors involved we may rarely enroll you in a shorter term plan on the lowest offer for the longest possible term.

14: Will there be any interruption when switching process occurs?

No, there will be no interruption in the flow of power. The switch is entirely a financial process.

15: Will I still get an electric bill?

Yes, you will continue to receive statements from the electricity providers we enroll you with. We opt you in for automatic payment and set up your account to match your preference.

16: Will you help me in the process of purchasing electricity rates for my business?

Yes! One of our expert agents will take you through the process of getting a competitive rate for your business. Contact us at contact@choicegenie.com

17: What is electric deregulation?

Deregulation separates the electric supply from electric delivery. Retail electric providers will provide electric supply.

18: Will I have to pay for the switch to Texas electricity providers?

No, if you currently have the power turned on in your name and you select a standard switch. You will have to pay an early termination charge if you switch providers more than 14 days prior to contract expiration.

19: When will my new service start after my switch to Texas electricity providers?

Typically switch occurs within one to two weeks. A priority move-in switch may result in additional TDSP charges on your first bill.

20: Do I need to notify my current Texas electricity provider if I choose to switch companies?

No, you do not need to notify your current Texas electricity. When a request for a change of service has been made, we will notify your current provider.

21: Whom do I call if I have an outage?

You should call the Transmission and Distribution Utility – or TDU for your area. Your electricity bill has the toll-free number mentioned.
